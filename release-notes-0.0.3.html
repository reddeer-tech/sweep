What's New

⚡ Optimize — see what's eating your CPU & RAM

Sweep now has a second mode next to disk cleaning. Flip the Clean | Optimize switch in the top bar and Sweep reads your Mac's live activity — then does what Activity Monitor doesn't: it tells you whose process that is and what to do about it.

  • Every process, attributed. All ~1,000+ processes are grouped under the app they belong to — a browser's dozens of renderers or an editor's helpers and language servers collapse into one row, with the developer's verified code-signing identity shown (e.g. Microsoft Corporation, Logitech Inc.), so mystery processes stop being a mystery.
  • Memory & CPU tabs. One engine, two rankings — real memory footprint (the number Activity Monitor shows) and live CPU%, plus a system header with used RAM, compressed memory, and load.
  • A verdict on every row. SAFE (fine to close), CAUTION (may cost you something — explained in plain language), or PROTECTED (core macOS — Sweep refuses to touch it, ever).
  • The right fix, not just a kill. Killing a process often does nothing — launchd restarts it, a login item brings it back, or its parent app respawns it. Sweep classifies why each thing runs and recommends the fix that actually sticks: quit the parent app, remove a login item, change a setting (e.g. exclude a folder from Spotlight instead of fighting the indexer), or — for orphaned leftovers — a safe force-quit.
  • One-click safe actions. Apps get a Quit button (graceful — you'll be prompted for unsaved work); orphaned processes get Force quit. Everything is confirmed first, nothing ever happens automatically, and the list re-checks itself after each action.
  • Make it stick. Third-party background jobs get Stop & disable — Sweep stops the process and disables its launchd job so it doesn't come back (your own login agents need no password; system daemons ask for admin once). Apps that relaunch at login get a one-click jump to System Settings ▸ Login Items. Spotlight re-indexing and a running Time Machine backup get their real fixes (Search Privacy / pause the backup) instead of a pointless kill.

Design refresh

  • Modern flat look across the app — dialogs, progress and controls now match the theme in both light and dark mode, and status badges adapt to the theme instead of assuming dark.

Notes

  • Optimize is macOS-only in this release.
  • Disk cleaning (the Clean tab) is unchanged.
